Copycat Olive Garden Minestrone Soup by Todd Wilbur

Clear soup,Pasta shells,Vegetable,Pasta, rice and grains,European,Winter,Savory,Seasonal,Novelty,Taste/mood,Stove top,Time to make,Equipment
Ingredients
  • olive oil
  • minced white onion (about 1 small onion)
  • chopped zucchini
  • frozen cut Italian cut green beans
  • minced celery (about 1/2 stalk)
  • minced garlic (about 4 cloves)
  • vegetable broth (Swanson is good *note ( Do not use chicken broth!*)
  • red kidney beans, drained
  • diced tomatoes
  • carrot (julienned or shredded)
  • minced fresh parsley
  • dried oregano
  • salt
  • ground black pepper
  • dried basil
  • dried thyme
  • hot water
  • fresh Baby Spinach
  • small shell pasta
Method
  1. Heat three tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat in a large soup pot.
  2. Saute onion, celery, garlic, green beans, and zucchini in the oil for 5 minutes or until onions begin to turn translucent.
  3. Add vegetable broth to pot, plus drained tomatoes, beans, carrot, hot water, and spices.
  4. Bring soup to a boil, then reduce heat and allow to simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Add spinach leaves and pasta and cook for an additional 20 minutes or until desired consistency.
  6. Makes about eight 1 1/2 cup servings.
